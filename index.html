Discover Our Research Group

Founded in 2021, the Indranil Maity Research Group is dedicated to advancing knowledge in nanoelectronics, gas sensor technology, and semiconductor device modeling through rigorous research and impactful publications. Our mission is to promote academic excellence and address pressing global challenges by integrating cutting-edge materials science, computational modeling, and device fabrication. Led by Dr. Indranil Maity, who holds a Ph.D. in Engineering from IIEST, Shibpur, our team specializes in electronic devices, 2D materials, VLSI design, and TCAD-based simulations. With extensive experience in DFT-based computational studies, nanomaterial characterization, and machine learning applications in medical diagnostics, we foster a culture of innovation, collaboration, and interdisciplinary research. Through our contributions to IEEE journals, international conferences, and advanced semiconductor studies, we strive to push the boundaries of sensor technology and nanoelectronics, ensuring a meaningful impact on both academia and industry. Join us as we drive scientific innovation and shape the future of next-generation electronic devices.

Dr. Indranil Maity

Bio

Dr. Indranil Maity – A Distinguished Researcher in Nanoelectronics and Gas Sensor Technology Dr. Indranil Maity is an accomplished researcher and educator specializing in Electronic Devices, Micro and Nanoelectronics Technology, Gas Sensors, 2D Materials, and VLSI Design. His contributions to the fields of semiconductor-based gas sensors, graphene derivatives, and nanotechnology have positioned him as a leading expert in the domain. Dr. Maity completed his Ph.D. in Engineering from the Indian Institute of Engineering Science and Technology (IIEST), Shibpur, where he focused on the Simulation and Experimental Validation of Functionalized Graphene-based Gas Sensor Devices. Prior to this, he earned his M.Tech in VLSI Design from the Institute of Radio Physics & Electronics, University of Calcutta, securing the second rank in his batch, and his B.Tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering from West Bengal University of Technology (WBUT). With a strong academic background, Dr. Maity has excelled in both research and teaching. He is currently an Associate Professor in the Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE) at the Institute of Engineering and Management (IEM), Kolkata. He has also served as an Assistant Professor at IEM and as a Visiting Faculty at Indira Gandhi Delhi Technical University for Women (IGDTUW), New Delhi. His international research exposure includes working as a Research Associate at Qatar University, focusing on DFT-based Computational Studies, and participating in the ILDP Study Abroad Program at Hiroshima University, Japan, where he researched Membrane-based Separation Processes. Dr. Maity’s research interests primarily lie in the design, fabrication, and characterization of gas sensor devices using semiconducting metal oxides and novel graphene derivatives. His work has led to numerous peer-reviewed journal publications, including papers in IEEE Transactions on Nanotechnology, IEEE Sensors Journal, and Springer Journals, among others. He has also contributed to international conferences and co-authored book chapters on 2D nanomaterials for healthcare applications. Throughout his career, Dr. Maity has received multiple awards and recognitions, including the IRSD Distinguished Young Scientist Award 2024 and the International Distinguished Young Researcher Award 2024. He was also honored with the Best Teacher Award at IEM, Kolkata. Additionally, his students have achieved prestigious awards under his guidance, such as the Director's Award for Best Scientific Mind and Best Student Contribution. Dr. Maity is an active IEEE member, serving as a reviewer for IEEE journals and a technical committee member for multiple IEEE conferences. He has chaired sessions at various international conferences and has been a panel judge for global research events. Beyond research, Dr. Maity has expertise in material synthesis, semiconductor characterization, and computational modeling using advanced simulation tools like QuantumATK, Silvaco TCAD, and MATLAB. His commitment to academic excellence, innovative research, and mentoring future scientists continues to make a significant impact in the field of nanoelectronics and sensor technology.

Expertise

Dr. Indranil Maity possesses extensive expertise across multiple domains, including Electronic Devices, Micro and Nanoelectronics Technology, Gas Sensors, 2D Materials, and VLSI Design. His specialization in semiconducting metal oxides and novel graphene derivatives for gas sensor device design, fabrication, and characterization has contributed significantly to advancements in sensor technology. With a strong foundation in nanotechnology and semiconductor materials, he has worked on DFT-based computational studies, spin-dependent electronic transport in graphene nanoribbons, and the selective tuning of oxygen functional groups for enhanced sensor performance. His research also includes device fabrication techniques such as sol-gel processing, hydrothermal synthesis, and electrochemical deposition, as well as material characterization methods like FESEM, Raman Spectroscopy, XPS, XRD, and AFM. In the field of VLSI technology, he has contributed to analog and digital circuit design using Cadence Virtuoso, SPICE simulations, and current mirror configurations. Dr. Maity has also explored 2D nanomaterials, such as graphene and transition metal dichalcogenides (MoS₂, MoSe₂), for their electronic and optoelectronic applications. His work in TCAD-based solar cell simulation has provided valuable insights into dual-junction and quantum dot solar cells. Additionally, his expertise extends to image processing applications in agricultural and medical diagnostics, utilizing RGB and HSV color space analysis for fruit maturity detection and cancerous cell classification through machine learning techniques. His technical proficiency spans QuantumATK, MATLAB, Origin, Latex, and various semiconductor parameter analysis tools. With a strong academic and research background, Dr. Maity has established himself as an expert in nanoelectronics, gas sensors, VLSI circuits, computational physics, and semiconductor technology, contributing to cutting-edge innovations in these fields.

Specializations

  • Electronic Devices: Focuses on the design, development, and application of semiconductor devices such as transistors, diodes, and integrated circuits, which form the backbone of modern electronic systems.
  • Micro and Nanoelectronics Technology: Involves the miniaturization of electronic circuits and components to enhance performance, energy efficiency, and integration for applications in computing and communication.
  • Gas Sensors: These sensors detect and measure gas concentrations in the environment using various sensing mechanisms, playing a crucial role in industrial safety, environmental monitoring, and healthcare.
  • 2D Materials: Ultra-thin materials like graphene and transition metal dichalcogenides (TMDs) exhibit unique electronic, optical, and mechanical properties, making them suitable for next-generation electronics and sensors.
  • VLSI Technology: Very-Large-Scale Integration (VLSI) is the process of integrating millions of transistors onto a single chip, enabling the development of high-performance microprocessors and digital circuits.
  • VLSI Design: Focuses on designing complex integrated circuits with optimized power, performance, and area constraints, widely used in processors, memory chips, and custom hardware accelerators.
  • Semiconducting Metal Oxide and Novel Graphene Derivatives Based Gas Sensor Device Design, Fabrication, and Characterization: Involves the development of high-sensitivity gas sensors using semiconducting metal oxides and graphene-based materials, aiming for improved selectivity and rapid detection capabilities.
  • Graphene and Other 2D Nanomaterials: These materials exhibit extraordinary electrical, mechanical, and thermal properties, making them ideal for flexible electronics, energy storage, and high-speed transistors.
  • Nanotechnology: This field manipulates matter at the atomic and molecular scale to develop novel materials and devices with enhanced functionalities for applications in medicine, electronics, and energy.
